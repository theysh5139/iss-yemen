# Enable 2-Step Verification for Gmail App Passwords

The error "The setting you are looking for is not available for your account" means you need to enable **2-Step Verification** first.

## Step 1: Enable 2-Step Verification

1. **Go to Google Account Security:**
   - Visit: https://myaccount.google.com/security
   - OR click "GO TO YOUR GOOGLE ACCOUNT" from the error page

2. **Find "2-Step Verification":**
   - Scroll down to the section "Signing in to Google"
   - Look for "2-Step Verification"
   - Click on it

3. **Start the Setup:**
   - Click "Get Started" button
   - Google will ask you to verify your identity

4. **Verify Your Phone Number:**
   - Enter your phone number
   - Choose how to receive the code:
     - Text message (SMS)
     - Phone call
   - Enter the verification code you receive
   - Click "Next"

5. **Turn On 2-Step Verification:**
   - Google will ask if you want to turn it on
   - Click "Turn On" or "Enable"

6. **Optional - Add Backup Options:**
   - You can add backup phone numbers
   - You can add backup email addresses
   - These help if you lose access to your main phone

## Why 2-Step Verification is Required?

Google requires 2-Step Verification for App Passwords because:
- It adds an extra layer of security
- App Passwords are less secure than OAuth (but easier to set up)
- It ensures only you can generate passwords for your account

## After Enabling 2-Step Verification

Once enabled, you'll:
- Need to verify with your phone when signing in from new devices
- Be able to generate App Passwords
- Have better account security overall
